About the Institute

Educating Parigam's children, one classroom at a time.

Parigam is one of the largest villages in Pulwama district, its name — Pari (fairy) and Gam (village) — drawn from local folklore. Surrounded by almond orchards, paddy fields and a quiet plateau, it is a community that has long valued learning alongside faith.

Mohammadia Educational Institute was founded to give the children of this village a school of their own: a place for solid academics, moral grounding, and the kind of attention that only a close-knit community can offer.

Academics

A clear path from first lessons to final exams.

Classes are structured across three stages, each building the foundation for the next — with English as the medium of instruction throughout.

Classes 1 – 5

Primary Section

Foundational literacy and numeracy, spoken English, and early Islamic studies in a nurturing, close-supervision setting.

Classes 6 – 8

Middle Section

Science, mathematics, social studies and languages deepen, alongside continued grounding in values and community life.

Classes 9 – 12

Senior Section

Board-exam preparation with subject streams, guidance, and a focus on the next step — college or career.
